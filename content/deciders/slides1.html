Teams ◈ Meet the Decision-Makers

Decisions must be made every single day.

But who is in the best position to make them?

The answer, of course, is …

It depends.

This session explores who should make the decision for the most important topics in the world of business.

Here are some options …

Government: A government's goal should be to ensure safety and prosperity of all its citizens.

Their decisions become laws which apply to everyone in the entire country.

Some decisions are best at this level. Others… not so much.

Unions: A union is a group of workers in the same industry.

They join together to ask for better things at their job, like more pay or safer conditions.

When workers speak together, they often have a stronger voice.

CEOs: A CEO is like the captain of a team.

They make big decisions for one company.

It's their job to make sure everything works well.

Individuals: Sometimes, letting the individual make their own decision is best.

The problem?

If everyone makes their own individual decision, there's no consistency for organizations.

Your job today: decide … who decides.

Before we start, discuss these four categories.

What are the pros and cons of each decision-maker?
