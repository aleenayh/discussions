Imagine your parents leave you and your sibling Jordan alone in a house.

While they're gone, a lamp breaks.

How did it happen? Well, it depends on who you ask.

This is a classic ethical scenario called The Prisoner's Dilemma.

If you both stay silent, the punishment will be less for both of you.

But if one sibling tells on the other, the one who spoke up will get off easy while the other will be punished more harshly.

In a few moments, you'll be placed in some different scenarios.

Each time, you'll have to make a decision:

Blame your sibling Jordan? Or stay silent and hope Jordan doesn't blame you?

Although you'll discuss your decisions with the room, your choices — and consequences — are yours alone.
