This week's topic explores a classic conflict:

Tradition: keeping things the same because they continue to work.

Innovation: looking to the future to improve the way things are.

Which approach is better?

It depends. Both approaches have risks.

Here's the situation:

We are the Executive Board of an educational company.

We want your group to design a brand new school.

Here are the rules:

Your school must be highly effective. We'll ask you to consider a few areas specifically.

In each area, you will choose between traditional approaches or innovative approaches.

Here's the catch.

All teams in this session will vote on each area. Your school plan will need to incorporate the option with the majority votes.

So all groups will build their school with the same constraints.

At the end, we'll ask you to pitch us your plan. You room will compete against other teams.

Which team will have the best pitch?

Since all teams will use the same school plan, it's not about what's included – it's about how you back those decisions up and explain your thinking. We're curious to see what you come up with.
